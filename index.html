Wole Soyinka

Nigeria's Best Playwright, Poet and Essayist

Soyinka has been a strong critic of successive Nigerian governments

Wole Soyinka's Timeline

  • 13 July 1934 - Akinwande Oluwole Babatunde Soyinka
  • 1954 - he attended Government College in Ibadan, and subsequently University College Ibadan and the University of Leeds in England.
  • 1965 - he seized the Western Nigeria Broadcasting Service studio and broadcast a demand for the cancellation of the Western Nigeria Regional Elections.
  • 1967 - during the Nigerian Civil War, he was arrested by the federal government of General Yakubu Gowon and put in solitary confinement for two years
  • 1975 - Soyinka was made a Professor of Comparative literature at the Obafemi Awolowo University, then called the University of Ife, up until 1999.
  • 1988 - 1991 - While in the United States, he first taught at Cornell University as Goldwin Smith professor for African Studies and Theatre Arts
  • 1996 - he was appointed Robert W. Woodruff Professor of the Arts at Emory University.
  • In 2008, He has also taught at the universities of Oxford, Harvard and Yale. Soyinka was also a Distinguished Scholar in Residence at Duke University
  • December 2017 - he was awarded the Europe Theatre Prize in the "Special Prize" category[15][16] awarded to someone who has "contributed to the realization of cultural events that promote understanding and the exchange of knowledge between peoples"
